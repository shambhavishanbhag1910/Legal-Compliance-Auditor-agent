IONWORKS ENERGY SERVICES
MANAGEMENT FINANCIAL REVIEW

Business Overview
IonWorks Energy Services operates across industrial technology, software-enabled services, and engineered products. Management monitors organic growth, backlog conversion, gross margin, operating cash flow, working capital, and customer retention when assessing performance.


Revenue and Operating Performance
Revenue for 2025 was $428.6 million compared with $389.2 million in 2024, an increase of 10.1%. Growth was driven by recurring software subscriptions and aftermarket services, partially offset by lower project revenue in the energy segment. Gross margin improved to 38.4% from 36.9% due to mix and procurement savings.

Material Risks
Results remain exposed to semiconductor availability, customer concentration in two end markets, foreign exchange volatility, cybersecurity events, supplier quality disruption, and delays in large customer projects. A prolonged production interruption at the primary electronics contract manufacturer could materially affect shipments and working capital.

Basis of Preparation
The financial information has been prepared in accordance with U.S. GAAP using accounting policies applied consistently with the prior year, except for newly adopted standards described in the notes. Management uses estimates for revenue recognition, warranty reserves, expected credit losses, inventory obsolescence, and impairment testing.

Liquidity and Capital Allocation
Management prioritizes operating liquidity, disciplined working-capital management, selective capital expenditure, and investment in product development. Capital allocation decisions consider leverage, covenant headroom, acquisition opportunities, and resilience under downside scenarios.

Operational Commentary
During the year, the company continued supplier dual-sourcing, service automation, and portfolio simplification initiatives. Management reviews backlog quality, order cancellation patterns, warranty trends, customer payment behavior, and supplier performance as part of its monthly operating process.

Non-GAAP Measures
Where management refers to adjusted measures, they are intended to supplement rather than replace the most directly comparable accounting measures. Definitions and reconciliations are maintained in management reporting schedules.
